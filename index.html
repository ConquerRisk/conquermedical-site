Medical AI Safety Research

Structural safety frameworks for clinical AI deployment

Conquer Medical develops formal methods to identify, quantify, and prevent structural failures in medical AI systems before they reach patients. Our research programme addresses the gap between AI validation and clinical safety.

Our Position

Medical AI is evaluated for discrimination and calibration. We argue it must also be evaluated for treatment-conditional validity: whether the variables required for a prediction to be clinically meaningful are available, correctly supplied, and structurally sufficient at the point of care. Our research provides the formal tools to make that evaluation possible.

Research Programme

AI-Based Hypothesis Adjudication

A structural framework for clinical inference, mechanistic testing, and safe medical AI deployment. Five foundational papers establishing the theory, diagnostics, and clinical protocols.

Paper 1

When the AI Predicts Progression and Treatment Brings Remission

Three structural failures prevent imaging AI from resolving treatment-conditioned tumour trajectory. Introduces Treatment Blindness Risk and Trajectory Indeterminacy Measure as pre-deployment diagnostics.

Preprint forthcoming
Paper 2

The AI Learned the Wrong Biology

Treatment-induced deformation defeats treatment-aware prediction. Introduces Curvature Shift and Deformation Risk as diagnostic quantities for detecting when the biological law itself has changed.

Preprint forthcoming
Paper 3 — Flagship

AI as Biological Hypothesis

The paradigm-defining paper. Proposes that AI predictions encode testable biological hypotheses, and introduces Hypothesis Discriminability as the measure of whether data can distinguish competing mechanisms.

Preprint forthcoming
Paper 4

Can Imaging Distinguish Why Treatment Fails?

Applies the AHA framework to competing resistance mechanisms in paediatric glioma. Establishes that mechanistic adjudication is carried by temporal trajectory structure, not local state-space separation.

Preprint forthcoming
Paper 5

Structured Evaluation Prevents Unnecessary Treatment Escalation

A clinical safety protocol based on pre-deployment diagnostic quantities. Introduces Structural SOAP and Extended SOAP as operational checklists for AI-assisted treatment monitoring.

Preprint forthcoming

Conceptual Architecture

Two layers of the research programme

AI-Based Hypothesis Adjudication

AHA — Epistemological Layer

The framework for asking: can observable data distinguish between competing biological hypotheses? Provides diagnostic quantities (TBR, TIM, CS, DR, HD) that answer this question formally, before any model is trained or any patient is scanned.

AI as Biological Primitive

ABP — Representational Layer

A multi-agent computational architecture in which each AI agent embodies a specific biological hypothesis. Interactions between agents encode mechanistic claims. Emergent system behaviour becomes the testable prediction. AHA provides the diagnostics; ABP provides the substrate.

Diagnostic Quantities

Pre-deployment safety tools

TBR
Treatment Blindness Risk

Maximum prediction error from ignoring treatment at a given tumour state. Answers: how much could I be misled?

TIM
Trajectory Indeterminacy Measure

Severity of treatment-input error. TIM > 1 means opposite-sign dynamics; TIM = 2 means equal-magnitude reversal.

CS
Curvature Shift

Detects whether treatment has changed the governing biological law, not just the trajectory under that law.

DR
Deformation Risk

Prediction error from using the pre-treatment law when the post-treatment law has deformed.

HD
Hypothesis Discriminability

Cohen's d between competing mechanistic hypotheses. Can the available data modality distinguish between them?

SOAP
Structural SOAP / Extended SOAP

Clinical protocol: State, Operators, Assessment of law, Plan. A safety checklist for AI-assisted treatment monitoring.
